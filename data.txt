<a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a>, Germany</div></div></div>\n<p><b>Road safety in Europe</b> encompasses transportation safety among road users in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Automobile_accident" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile accident">automobile accidents</a>, pedestrian or cycling accidents, motor-coach accidents, and other incidents occurring within the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> or within the European region of the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> (49 countries). Road traffic safety refers to the methods and measures used to prevent road users from being killed or seriously injured.\n</p><p>In 2016, according to the World Health Organisation, road accidents were the eighth-biggest cause of death in the world; deadlier than both diarrhoeal diseases and tuberculosis.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Not only is it important to consider road fatalities, but for every fatality on Europe\'s roads, it is estimated that 4 people will become permanently disabled, 10 will suffer <a href="/wiki/Brain_injuries" class="mw-redirect" title="Brain injuries">brain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Spinal_cord_damage" class="mw-redirect" title="Spinal cord damage">spinal cord damage</a>, 10 people will be seriously injured and 40 will have sustained minor injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-europa1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On top of this, road accidents incur a large economic impact. In Europe alone, it is estimated that road accidents are a cost to society by a measure of €130 billion annually.<sup id="cite_ref-europa1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Road accidents and incidents happen for a number of reasons. The main cause of an accident is speed, this is followed by other issues such as driving whilst under the influence of drink or drugs, being distracted at the wheel by mobile devices, in-car radios or personal navigation devices.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> These risk factors listed here are but a few reasons for road collisions to occur and they demonstrate the myriad of complex factors that are at play for road-safety policy makers.\n</p>\n<div id="toc" class="toc" role="navigation" aria-labelledby="mw-toc-heading"><input European Union (EU) has the safest roads in the world; in which 49 people per million inhabitants died in a road collision in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-europa2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the year 2000 over 50,000 people in Europe lost their lives on the roads, by 2009 this number had been reduced to over 35,000; and by 2018 the figure has been reduced further to 25,100, whilst in the same year the number of serious injuries incurred as a result of road collisions was 135,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-europa2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The European Commission (EC) has laid out a plan entitled <i>Vision Zero</i> which endeavours to reduce the incidence of road induced fatalities to a rate of zero by the year 2050.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In order to be able to achieve their long-term ambition of zero deaths on Europe\'s roads, the Commission implemented a road safety strategy, <i>The Road Safety Programme</i>, in which they aimed to halve the number of fatalities caused by road accidents and incidents between 2011 and 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-europa1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As of 2018, European Union member states are far from this target, since there has only been a 20% reduction in road fatalities, which makes the target of a 50% reduction by 2020 now seem implausible.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> \n</p>\n<table>\n<tbody><tr>\n<td><style organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Health Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The World Health Organization issued a European Status Report on road safety.<sup id="cite_ref-who.int_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-who.int-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Ninety per cent of WHO countries have a safety agency<sup id="cite_ref-who.int_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-who.int-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> operating with their respective transport ministries, except in CIS countries where the topic falls under the interior minister.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_an_EU_perspective">From an EU perspective</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: From an EU perspective">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Road safety policy making in the EU falls jointly on the European institutions and member states; and it is the European Commission (EC) which has a particularly important role in overseeing road safety policy across the Union. This is because it has oversight over product standards and regulations, as well as certain aspects of infrastructure development and management. Road safety is based upon the EU principle of <a href="/wiki/Subsidiarity" title="Subsidiarity">subsidiarity</a>: national and local authorities are responsible for most decisions, including enforcement and awareness-raising, while the EU operates a general framework for improved road safety via legislation and recommendations e.g. introducing minimum safety requirements for the Trans-European Transport Networks, and technical requirements for the transport of dangerous goods.<sup id="cite_ref-europa.eu_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa.eu-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The EU publishes various legal texts regarding road safety.<sup id="cite_ref-europa.eu1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa.eu1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Road_Safety_NGOs">European Road Safety NGOs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: European Road Safety NGOs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://etsc.eu/">European Transport Safety Council</a> is an NGO based in Brussels. It aims to reduce the numbers of deaths and injuries in transport in Europe. The Council reported an increase in fatalities in most European countries in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definitions">Definitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Definitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Killed_definition">Killed definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Killed definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg/220px-General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg/330px-General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg/440px-General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="960" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:General_Patton%27s_grave_300806.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>U.S. Army General <a href="/wiki/George_S._Patton" title="George S. This definition has been adopted across most EU countries.\n</p><p>Some countries have applied this definition retrospectively where possible. For instance, until 2004 France counted its killed at six days, but in an effort to enable comparison with neighbor countries a multiplicative coefficient 1.057 was used up to 2004 and 1.069 since 2005 to convert the killed at six days into killed at thirty days, before France adopted the international definition in 2005.\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Injuries">Injuries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Injuries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Each year road crashes generate about 120,000 fatalities and 2.4 million injuries in the European region of the World Health Organization. Road traffic injuries are the leading cause of death among adolescents and young adults.<sup id="cite_ref-who.int_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-who.int-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In 2015, the European Commission published a figure for the number of people seriously injured on Europe\'s roads: 135,000 people in 2014. To obtain this figure all countries of the EU needed to align on a common standardized medical definition of what constitutes a serious road injury.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>In Europe, for every person killed in traffic crashes, many more suffer serious injuries with life-changing consequences.\n</p><p>Serious injuries are more costly to society because of the long-term rehabilitation and healthcare needed. Vulnerable road users, such us pedestrians, cyclists, motorcyclists or elderly road users, are especially concerned.<sup id="cite_ref-europa.eu_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europa.eu-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>Between 2010 and 2018, between 206 and 222 thousands serious injured were counted yearly for 23 EU members<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>\n</p>\n<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_issues">Other issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Other issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>The level of transport-related air pollution is also a major public health concern in most countries of Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-who.int_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-who.int-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p class="mw-empty-elt">\n</p>\n<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_road_casualties_indicators">Main road casualties indicators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Main road casualties indicators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>\n<div class="mw-kartographer-container thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width: 300px;"><a class="mw-kartographer-map" style="width: 300px; class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Road_safety_in_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Rating roads for safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>\n<p>Since 1999 the <a href="/wiki/EuroRAP" title="EuroRAP">EuroRAP</a> initiative has been assessing major roads in Europe with a road protection score. This results in a star rating for roads based on how well its design would protect car occupants from being severely injured or killed if a head-on, run-off, or intersection accident occurs, with four stars representing a road with the best survivability features.<sup id="cite_ref-eurorap_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eurorap-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The scheme states it has highlighted thousands of road sections across Europe where road users are routinely maimed and killed for want of safety features, sometimes for little more than the cost of safety fencing or the paint required to improve road markings.<sup id="cite_ref-eurorap2_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eurorap2-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>There are plans to extend the measurements to rate the probability of an accident for the road. These ratings are being used to inform planning and authorities\' targets. For example, in Britain two thirds of all road deaths in Britain happen on rural roads, which score badly when compared with the high-quality motorway network; single carriageways claim 80% of rural deaths and serious injuries, while 40% of rural car occupant casualties are in cars that hit roadside objects, such as trees. Improvements in driver training and safety features for rural roads are hoped to reduce this statistic.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>\n</p><p>The number of designated traffic officers in the UK fell from 15 to 20% of police force strength in 1966 to seven per cent of force strength in 1998, and between 1999 and 2004 by 21%.<sup id="cite_ref-policeforce_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-policeforce-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> It is an item of debate whether the reduction in
